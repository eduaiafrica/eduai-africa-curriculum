What This Is
This repository contains the complete, open-source curriculum for EduAI Africa — a WhatsApp-native AI literacy programme delivering free education to Uganda's students, teachers, community members, farmers, and frontline professionals.
First cohort launched: 12th March 2026.
Delivery: WhatsApp — no app downloads, no devices, no fees.
Community access: Free, permanently.
Uganda's 2026 General Elections concluded on 4th February 2026. During the campaign period, AI-generated deepfake videos, fabricated voice recordings, and algorithmically amplified disinformation spread widely across Ugandan WhatsApp networks. The communities targeted during those elections remain exposed to the same AI manipulation tools today — in health misinformation, economic scams, and community conflict.
EduAI Africa was built to close that gap. This curriculum is our contribution to every community that deserves to understand the technology shaping their world.

How to Use This Curriculum
For Schools and Teachers
Download any of the bot-playbooks/ documents. The full lesson content is inside — written in plain English, structured week by week, and designed to be delivered on WhatsApp or adapted for classroom use without any additional technology.

Import any file directly into Typebot to get a fully structured conversational bot with all messages, branching logic, and quizzes already built. Connect to WhatsApp Cloud API (free up to 1,000 conversations/month) to deploy immediately.

The Deepfake Detection Framework
At the core of every EduAI Africa track is a simple, phone-accessible deepfake detection method we call the Three-Check System:
👁️ CHECK 1 — THE BLINK TEST
Real humans blink every 4–6 seconds. AI-generated video faces often do not blink naturally. Watch the eyes of any suspicious video and count the blinks.
✂️ CHECK 2 — THE EDGE TEST
Look at the hairline and jawline of the face in the video. AI-generated faces often have a slight blur or unnatural smoothness at the edges. Real faces are sharp and consistent everywhere.
📰 CHECK 3 — THE NEWS TEST
If something truly shocking is happening, it would be on NTV Uganda, Daily Monitor, or BBC. If a video is only circulating on WhatsApp and cannot be found on any trusted news source — it is almost certainly fake.
These three checks require no technology, no training, and no internet. They work on any phone. They are the foundation of AI literacy for communities that cannot wait.
Curriculum Principles
1. WhatsApp-native
Every lesson is designed for delivery through WhatsApp — the platform 22 million Ugandans already use every day. No downloads. No new apps. No data infrastructure beyond basic mobile data.
2. Competency-Based
The curriculum is aligned to Uganda's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework. Teachers can integrate AI literacy into existing lesson delivery without additional burden.
3. Community-first, not technology-first
Every example, every scenario, and every challenge is grounded in the lived reality of Ugandan communities — Nakasero market vendors, Boda riders, P6 students, UPDF cadets, and smallholder farmers. This is not a Silicon Valley curriculum translated for Africa. It was built here.
4. Permanently free for community learners
Students, public school teachers, and community members access all content free, forever. Professional certification and organisational licensing support programme sustainability without compromising community access.
5. Open source
All curriculum content is published here for independent verification, adaptation, and use. We believe the knowledge that protects communities from AI manipulation belongs to those communities — not behind a paywall.
